# Copy Length Reference

Optimal lengths for every content format. Use as a quick lookup during writing.

---

## Social Media

| Format | Min | Optimal | Max | Notes |
|--------|-----|---------|-----|-------|
| LinkedIn post | 800 chars | 1,300–2,000 chars | 3,000 chars | Sweet spot: 1,500 chars |
| LinkedIn carousel slide | 20 words | 30–50 words | 80 words | 1 idea per slide |
| Tweet (single) | 50 chars | 200–270 chars | 280 chars | Leave 10-char buffer |
| Thread tweet | 100 chars | 200–270 chars | 280 chars | Each must stand alone |
| Thread length | 3 tweets | 6–10 tweets | 15 tweets | Quality > quantity |
| Instagram caption | 100 chars | 500–1,000 chars | 2,200 chars | First line is the hook |

---

## Email

| Element | Min | Optimal | Max | Notes |
|---------|-----|---------|-----|-------|
| Subject line | 20 chars | 30–50 chars | 60 chars | Under 50 = higher open rate |
| Preview text | 30 chars | 40–90 chars | 140 chars | Extends subject, no repeat |
| Nurture email body | 100 words | 150–300 words | 400 words | Respect inbox time |
| Launch email body | 200 words | 400–600 words | 800 words | More detail justified |
| Welcome email | 150 words | 200–350 words | 500 words | Short, warm, clear next step |
| P.S. line | 10 words | 15–30 words | 50 words | Often most-read line |

---

## Ad Copy

| Element | Min | Optimal | Max | Notes |
|---------|-----|---------|-----|-------|
| Facebook primary text | 40 chars | 80–125 chars | 250 chars | 125 visible without "see more" |
| Facebook headline | 15 chars | 25–40 chars | 40 chars | Benefit-driven |
| Facebook description | 10 chars | 20–30 chars | 30 chars | Urgency or clarification |
| Google Ad headline | 10 chars | 25–30 chars | 30 chars | Hard limit, count carefully |
| Google Ad description | 40 chars | 70–90 chars | 90 chars | Expand on headline promise |
| Display ad headline | 15 chars | 20–30 chars | 30 chars | Brand + benefit |
| Display ad body | 30 chars | 60–80 chars | 90 chars | One sentence max |

---

## Landing Pages

| Element | Min | Optimal | Max | Notes |
|---------|-----|---------|-----|-------|
| Headline | 4 words | 6–10 words | 12 words | Must pass 5-second test |
| Subheadline | 1 sentence | 2–3 sentences | 4 sentences | Who + what + how fast |
| CTA button | 2 words | 2–4 words | 6 words | Action verb + outcome |
| Feature description | 15 words | 25–40 words | 60 words | Benefit before feature |
| Testimonial | 15 words | 30–50 words | 80 words | Specific result + emotion |
| FAQ answer | 20 words | 40–80 words | 120 words | Direct answer first |
| Section heading | 3 words | 4–8 words | 10 words | Benefit, not label |
| Meta description | 120 chars | 150–160 chars | 160 chars | For SEO/social sharing |
| Total page word count | 500 words | 1,500–3,000 words | 5,000 words | Depends on offer complexity |

## Other Formats

| Format | Min | Optimal | Max | Notes |
|--------|-----|---------|-----|-------|
| Product description | 50 words | 100–200 words | 300 words | Benefits first, specs second |
| Tagline/slogan | 2 words | 3–7 words | 10 words | Memorable > descriptive |
| Brand positioning | 1 sentence | 2–3 sentences | 5 sentences | Internal clarity doc |
| Case study summary | 100 words | 200–400 words | 600 words | Result in first sentence |
| Press release headline | 8 words | 10–15 words | 20 words | Newsworthy + specific |
| Bio (social) | 50 chars | 100–150 chars | 160 chars | Role + proof + personality |

---

## Rules of Thumb

1. **When in doubt, shorter.** Cut 20% and see if anything was lost.
2. **Length follows complexity.** Simple offer = short page. Complex offer = long page.
3. **First sentence is always the shortest.** Hook with brevity.
4. **Every word must earn its place.** If cutting it doesn't change meaning, cut it.
5. **Platform limits are creative constraints, not obstacles.** 280 chars forces clarity.
