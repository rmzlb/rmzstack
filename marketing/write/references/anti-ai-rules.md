# Anti-AI Writing Rules

This is the writing reference for content-writer. For the detection/scoring reference,
see `roast/references/ai-detection.md`.

This file focuses on HOW TO WRITE to avoid AI patterns — not just what to avoid.

---

## The One-Sentence Rule

**If a sentence could appear in 100 other posts on the same topic, cut it.**

The goal isn't to avoid AI detection. The goal is to say something true and specific
that only you could say. That automatically passes detection.

---

## Typography Rules

| Rule | Applies to |
|------|-----------|
| No em-dash (—) | All formats |
| No bullet lists in prose body | LinkedIn, email, Twitter |
| No arrow lists (→) | All formats |
| No bold headers mid-post | LinkedIn, Twitter, email |
| 0-1 exclamation marks per piece | All formats |

**When you need to show a list in prose:** Use commas, semicolons, or separate sentences.

❌ "Three things helped: → Consistency → Clarity → Volume"
✅ "Three things helped. Consistency (posting every day, even bad days). Clarity (one idea per post). Volume (I wrote 200 posts before any got traction)."

---

## How to Replace Poison Words

Don't just delete them. Find what you actually mean.

| Poison word | What you might mean | Replace with |
|-------------|--------------------|-----------  |
| leverage | use strategically | "use", "get more out of", "turn X into Y" |
| foster | encourage slowly | "build", "create conditions for" |
| enhance | make better | "improve", "speed up", describe HOW it improves |
| streamline | make faster | "cut X steps to Y", describe the specific improvement |
| robust | strong/reliable | "handles [specific thing]", "won't break when [specific condition]" |
| holistic | comprehensive | describe the PARTS that make it complete |
| synergy | combined value | describe WHAT the combination produces specifically |
| journey | experience over time | "12 months of [specific thing]", tell the actual story |
| landscape | environment | describe the specific environment with real examples |
| paradigm | model/way of thinking | describe the specific model or switch |
| cutting-edge | new | "released [date]", "1 of 3 tools that do [specific thing]" |
| unprecedented | unusually high/new | use the actual numbers or the actual first |
| pivotal | important for X | describe WHY it mattered with specific consequence |

## How to Fix Structural AI Patterns

### Problem: Uniform sentence length

**Before:**
> "Building an audience takes time. Consistency is the most important factor.
> Most people give up too early. The compound effect is real."
(13, 11, 10, 8 words — too uniform)

**After:**
> "Building an audience takes longer than anyone admits. Most people quit at month 3.
> I know because I've had the month 4 conversation with dozens of founders who
> were 30 days from their first real traction."
(mix of 8, 7, 29 words)

**How to fix:** After writing, look at 5 consecutive sentences. If none vary by more
than 6 words, rewrite at least one to be very short (3-5 words) or very long (25+).

---

### Problem: The contrast cliché

**Before:**
> "It's not about the algorithm. It's about the people."

**After:**
> "The algorithm stuff matters. But I grew 4x when I stopped optimizing for it."
(specific result, specific behavior change)

**Rule:** The "It's not X. It's Y." structure is allowed once per piece. Never twice.

---

### Problem: AI starts sentences

**Before:**
> "It is crucial to understand that consistency plays a vital role.
> Furthermore, authenticity drives genuine engagement.
> Moreover, the algorithm rewards regular posting behavior."

**After (rewrite completely):**
> "Consistency matters. Not because the algorithm rewards it — though it does —
> but because you get better. Post 200 times and your 200th post will be
> unrecognizable from your first."

---

### Problem: Template endings

**Before:**
> "The best time to start was yesterday. The second best time is today."
> "Done is better than perfect."
> "Start before you're ready."

These are not endings. They're bumper stickers. They signal the writer stopped thinking.

**Better endings:**
- A specific question that invites real responses
- An honest admission: "I still don't know if this was the right call."
- A loop opener: "Next time I'll try [thing]. I'll let you know."
- A reframe: Put the last thought differently than the first

## The 5 Writing Moves That Sound Human

### Move 1: The Specific Recall

Instead of: "I learned that networking matters."
Write: "I got my first client because a college friend forwarded my email to a founder
she'd met once. Three months later, that founder was writing me a check."

---

### Move 2: The Honest Parenthetical

Instead of: "This is hard."
Write: "This is hard (and I'm saying that after doing it successfully, so imagine how
hard it was when I was still failing)."

---

### Move 3: The Short Brake

Mid-paragraph, one short sentence. Then continue.
> "I shipped the MVP. It was embarrassing. I shipped it anyway."

---

### Move 4: The Against-Expectation Number

Instead of: "Most users stayed."
Write: "73% of users were still active 6 months later. The industry average is 12%."
(your number + context = credibility)

---

### Move 5: The Reluctant Admission

> "I was wrong about this for 2 years. The thing I thought was a feature turned out
> to be the reason people churned."

Nothing signals real experience like admitting you were wrong about something specific.

## Self-Check Before Submitting

Run through this in 2 minutes before handing a draft to roast:

```
ANTI-AI CHECK (2 min)
[ ] No em-dash used anywhere
[ ] No Tier 1 poison words
[ ] No burned phrases ("It's important to note", "In today's digital age", etc.)
[ ] Sentence lengths vary — find the shortest and longest sentence
[ ] At least 1 contraction
[ ] At least 1 specific number
[ ] At least 1 moment of self-deprecation or admitting uncertainty
[ ] Ending is NOT a motivational kicker / perfect aphorism
[ ] No "It's not X. It's Y." used more than once
[ ] First 3 sentences: read aloud — do they sound like a person?
```

2-3 failures: note them, proceed to roast (they'll flag).
4+ failures: rewrite before auditing.
