# Headline Formulas

40+ tested headline patterns for every format and use case.
Reference during writing phase. Use as a starting point, not a template to fill in.

---

## LinkedIn Hook Formulas

### Ultra-Short Statement (2-5 words)
The most effective LinkedIn hook. Creates a gap the reader needs to close.

```
"I almost quit."
"This failed completely."
"I was wrong."
"Nobody told me this."
"We almost didn't make it."
"6 months. Nothing."
"It worked. I panicked."
```

**Rule:** Ultra-short hooks require a high-tension second line. Don't leave readers hanging on nothing.

### Number Lead
Establishes credibility and sets expectations.

```
"[N] months ago, I [past state]."
"[N] users. [N] emails. [N] days."
"I've talked to [N] [type of people]. Here's what they all say."
"[Year]. [One sentence establishing stakes]."
```

**Avoid:** "[N] things I learned" as the hook — it promises a list, which signals AI.

### Observation Lead
Something specific you noticed that most people miss.

```
"Nobody talks about what happens after [milestone]."
"Every [type of person] I know does this wrong."
"The [common thing] that actually [surprising effect]."
"Something changed in [month]. Nobody's written about it."
```

### Contrarian Lead
Open with the conventional wisdom you're about to challenge.

```
"[Popular advice] is overrated."
"Stop [thing everyone does]."
"[Thing people celebrate] is a trap."
"The [widely recommended thing] doesn't work. Here's what does."
```

**Rule:** You need evidence for this to work. Don't be contrarian without proof.

## Landing Page Headlines

### Formula 1: Benefit + Speed
```
"[Do X] in [time] — [secondary benefit]"
"Launch your [thing] in [time]"
"[Result] without [common obstacle]"
```

Examples:
- "Deploy your first app in 10 minutes"
- "Write a month of content in 2 hours"
- "Get 10 customer interviews in a week"

---

### Formula 2: The Pain Killer
```
"Stop [specific frustrating thing]"
"No more [painful experience]"
"[Painful scenario]? [Solution exists]."
```

Examples:
- "Stop losing customers at checkout"
- "No more 3am deployments"
- "Chasing invoices? Not anymore."

**Note:** The pain must be felt viscerally. "Stop inefficient workflows" is weak.
"Stop rebuilding the same integration for every new client" is specific.

---

### Formula 3: The Identity Headline
```
"For [specific person] who [specific situation]"
"Built for [specific type of person]"
"The [tool/service] [specific person] actually uses"
```

Examples:
- "For founders who hate writing but need to post"
- "Built for solo engineers who want to ship"
- "The design tool designers actually use"

**Why it works:** Niche targeting signals you actually understand the user.

---

### Formula 4: The Transformation
```
"From [before state] to [after state]"
"Turn [problem] into [outcome]"
"[Before] → [After] in [timeframe]"
```

Examples:
- "From idea to live product in a weekend"
- "Turn cold outreach into warm conversations"
- "From 0 to 1,000 subscribers in 90 days"

**Rule:** Before/after must be concrete. "From chaos to clarity" is not a transformation.

---

### Formula 5: The Simple Promise
```
"[Specific result] or [guarantee]"
"[Exactly what it is] for [exactly who it's for]"
"[Result] in [time/steps]. Guaranteed."
```

Examples:
- "100 beta users or your money back"
- "SEO audits for e-commerce founders"
- "Working prototype in 5 days"

## Email Subject Lines

### Curiosity Gap
```
"The [thing] I stopped using (and why)"
"What [impressive person/company] got wrong"
"This [timeframe] experiment changed how I [activity]"
"I tried [thing] for [N] days. Here's what actually happened."
```

### Personal
```
"I was wrong about [thing you believe in]"
"The mistake that cost me [specific loss]"
"Honest update: [timeframe] in"
"What I'd do differently if I started today"
```

### Benefit
```
"How to [specific result] in [specific timeframe]"
"[N] [things] that [specific outcome] (with examples)"
"The [framework/system] that [specific people] use to [outcome]"
```

### Urgency
```
"[Access] closes [specific time]"
"[Price] goes up [specific date]"
"[N] spots left (not a sales tactic — literally [N])"
"This is the last email about [thing]"
```

### Social Proof
```
"[N] [people] did [thing]. Here's what worked."
"[Impressive person] shared their [thing]. We dug into it."
"How [company/person] [achieved result] (breakdown)"
```

## Ad Copy Headlines

### Facebook/Instagram (40 chars)
```
"[Result] without [obstacle]"
"Why [target audience] use [thing]"
"Get [result] in [timeframe]"
"[Number]+ [audience] can't be wrong"
"Stop [painful thing]. Start [good thing]."
```

### Google Ads (30 chars — count carefully)
```
Benefit:      "[Verb] [thing] [N]% faster"
Feature:      "[Product] for [audience]"
Social proof: "Join [N]+ [audience]"
Action:       "Try [thing] free today"
Urgency:      "[N]% off ends [day]"
Question:     "Tired of [specific pain]?"
```

**30-char examples:**
- "Build APIs 10x faster" (22)
- "Analytics for founders" (23)
- "Join 3,000+ engineers" (22)
- "Free trial, no card" (20)

## What Makes Headlines Fail

**Too vague:**
- "Transform your business" → Transform it HOW?
- "Better results" → Better than what?
- "The power of consistency" → This says nothing

**Too clever:**
- "Unlock the secret sauce of engagement alchemy" → What is this
- "Navigate the content landscape" → Burned phrases + vague

**Too safe:**
- "Content marketing tips" → No reason to click
- "Learn about our product" → No benefit stated
- "The ultimate guide to [topic]" → Doesn't promise anything specific

**The 5-second test:** Show the headline to someone unfamiliar with your product.
After 5 seconds, ask: "What does this do? Who is it for? Why should you care?"
If they can't answer all three, the headline needs work.
