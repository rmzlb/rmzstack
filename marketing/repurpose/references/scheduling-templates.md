# Publishing Schedule Templates

Stagger repurposed content for maximum reach. Never publish everything at once.

---

## Why Stagger?

1. **Different audiences check different platforms on different days**
2. **Algorithmic fatigue**: posting the same idea everywhere at once = less reach each
3. **You can learn**: Day 0 LinkedIn results inform how you frame the Day 1 X thread
4. **Longer content lifespan**: 7 days of content from 1 idea vs. 1 day of spam

---

## Template 1: Weekly Content Sprint (LinkedIn Primary)

Best for: personal brand builders, founders, solo marketers.

```
Day 0 (Tue):  LinkedIn original post (8am local, primary audience)
Day 1 (Wed):  X/Twitter thread (adapted hook, same core insight)
Day 2 (Thu):  Best single tweet from the thread (the quotable line)
Day 3 (Fri):  Email to list (personal angle, deeper story)
Day 5 (Mon):  LinkedIn carousel (visual framework from the post)
Day 7 (Wed):  LinkedIn post #2 (new angle on same topic, references don't reread)
```

**Why Tuesday start?** LinkedIn engagement peaks Tue-Thu morning.
Email performs best Tue-Thu. This schedule hits both peaks.

---

## Template 2: Blog Launch Sprint

Best for: content marketing teams, SEO-driven blogs.

```
Day 0:   Blog post published (SEO-optimized, 2000+ words)
Day 0:   LinkedIn post #1 (hook from blog intro)
Day 1:   X/Twitter thread (5 key points from blog)
Day 2:   Email to list (link to blog with personal intro)
Day 3:   LinkedIn post #2 (different section, standalone angle)
Day 5:   3 standalone tweets (quotable lines from blog)
Day 7:   LinkedIn post #3 (contrarian take from blog)
Day 10:  Email #2 (follow-up with most popular section)
Day 14:  Carousel (visual summary of blog framework)
```

**Why 14-day spread?** Blog content is evergreen. Social is ephemeral.
Stretching maximizes the blog's promotional window.

## Template 3: Video/Podcast Episode

Best for: creators with video/audio content.

```
Day -1:   Teaser clip on X + LinkedIn (15-30 sec, best moment)
Day 0:    Full episode live (YouTube/podcast platform)
Day 0:    LinkedIn post (key insight from episode, link in comment)
Day 1:    X thread (3-5 key takeaways from episode)
Day 2:    Blog post (written version, SEO value)
Day 3:    Email to list (behind-the-scenes of the episode)
Day 4-6:  3 short clips on X/LinkedIn/Instagram (15-60 sec each)
Day 7:    Carousel on LinkedIn (visual summary of episode)
Day 10:   Follow-up post ("people asked about X from last week's episode...")
```

## Template 4: Product Launch / Case Study

Best for: launching features, sharing customer wins.

```
Day -7:   Teaser LinkedIn post ("something's coming...")
Day -3:   Behind-the-scenes post (building process)
Day 0:    Launch post (LinkedIn + X + email blast)
Day 1:    Customer story post (the case study, human angle)
Day 2:    X thread (how it works, technical for dev audience)
Day 3:    Email #2 (to non-openers, different subject)
Day 5:    Results post (early numbers, user feedback)
Day 7:    Lessons learned post (what surprised you)
Day 14:   Comparison post (your product vs. alternatives)
```

## Spacing Rules

| Platforms involved | Minimum gap | Why |
|-------------------|-------------|-----|
| LinkedIn → LinkedIn | 2 days | Same audience, don't fatigue them |
| LinkedIn → X | 1 day | Different audience, light overlap |
| LinkedIn → Email | 1-3 days | Email subscribers may follow you on LinkedIn |
| X → X (different format) | 1 day | Thread today, single tweet tomorrow |
| Any → Carousel | 3-5 days | Carousel references original — give it time |
| Any → Video | 2-3 days | Video feels fresh even if idea isn't new |

### When to Break the Rules
- **Product launch**: Compress to 3-day sprint (urgency justifies frequency)
- **Viral post**: Immediately repurpose to X if LinkedIn goes viral (ride the wave)
- **Timely topic**: Skip scheduling, post everywhere same day (relevance > strategy)

## Tracking Which Version Performs

After each repurpose cycle, log:

```yaml
source: "[Original platform and format]"
published: "YYYY-MM-DD"
repurposed:
  - platform: linkedin-carousel
    date: "YYYY-MM-DD"
    impressions: 
    engagement_rate: 
    notes: ""
  - platform: x-thread
    date: "YYYY-MM-DD"
    impressions: 
    engagement_rate: 
    notes: ""
```

After 5+ cycles, patterns appear:
- "X threads consistently get 3x the engagement of single tweets"
- "Email drives more DMs than any social platform"
- "Carousels underperform for my audience — stop making them"

Feed this data into `marketing-analytics` for systematic analysis.
