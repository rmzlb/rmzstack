# Platform Adaptation Rules

Not "make it shorter." Real adaptation: tone, hook, structure, CTA, algorithm.

---

## LinkedIn

### Algorithm Context
- First 60-90 minutes after posting = critical engagement window
- Author replies to comments = strongest positive signal
- External links in post body = reach penalty (put links in first comment)
- Dwell time matters: longer reads = more distribution
- Engagement from outside your network = viral signal

### Adaptation Rules
- **Tone**: Professional-personal. You're a builder sharing what you learned.
- **Hook**: Ultra-short (2-5 words). Statement, not question. Period at the end.
- **Structure**: Short paragraphs (1-3 sentences). Lots of white space. Blank lines between every paragraph.
- **Numbers**: Always specific. "7 months" not "several months."
- **Self-deprecation**: Plays very well. LinkedIn rewards vulnerability from builders.
- **CTA**: Open question at the end. "What's your experience?" > "Follow me for more."
- **Hashtags**: 0-3 max. At the end, not inline. Only use relevant ones with real volume.
- **Length sweet spot**: 1,300-2,000 characters (including spaces).
- **Posting time**: Weekdays 7-9am local time of your target audience.

### What NOT to Do on LinkedIn
- Don't use arrow lists (→). AI signal.
- Don't use emojis as bullet points (🔥 📌 💡). Screams template.
- Don't "agree?" at the end. Low-effort engagement bait.
- Don't repost your tweet as-is. Too short, no context, looks lazy.

## X/Twitter

### Algorithm Context
- Quote RT > RT for reach
- First self-reply (add context, link, or thread) helps
- Images boost engagement ~35%
- Don't delete and repost: kills the original tweet
- Engagement in first 30 min = distribution signal
- Bookmarks are a strong positive signal (save-worthy content)

### Adaptation Rules
- **Tone**: Sharp, direct, opinionated. Hot takes welcome. Less context than LinkedIn.
- **Hook**: Must stop scroll in first 5 words. No preamble.
- **Structure**: Thread = tweet 1 is the hook (strongest claim). Each tweet must stand alone AND connect to the thread.
- **Numbers**: Even more critical than LinkedIn. "After 18 months" > "After a long time."
- **Opinions**: Stronger than LinkedIn. "This is wrong" > "There are different perspectives."
- **CTA**: Retweet request, follow, or open question. "Save this for later" works (bookmarks = signal).
- **Hashtags**: 0-1 max. Hashtags on X look desperate. Let the content speak.
- **Length**: Single tweet: 200-270 chars. Thread: 5-12 tweets.
- **Posting time**: Weekdays 8-10am and 6-8pm local time.

### Thread-Specific Rules
- Tweet 1: Hook + claim. No "Thread 🧵" — just start strong.
- Tweet 2: Context or "why this matters"
- Tweets 3-N: Evidence, examples, steps (one idea per tweet)
- Second-to-last: Counter-intuitive insight or twist
- Last: Summary + CTA + optional link

### What NOT to Do on X
- Don't cross-post LinkedIn content verbatim (too long, wrong tone)
- Don't use "Here's what I learned:" as a thread opener (played out)
- Don't thread more than 12 tweets (reader drops off)
- Don't use LinkedIn's blank-line spacing (looks weird on X)

## Email

### Adaptation Rules
- **Tone**: Most personal of all platforms. 1-on-1 conversation. Reader should feel like you wrote it just for them.
- **Hook**: Subject line IS the hook. Curiosity gap or personal angle. 30-50 chars.
- **Structure**: Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences). Lots of white space. ONE CTA.
- **Preview text**: Extends the subject line. Not a repeat. 40-90 chars.
- **"You" and "I"**: Use both frequently. Every paragraph should have one.
- **CTA**: One link, one action. Don't give 3 options.
- **P.S.**: Second most-read element after subject line. Use for bonus value or secondary CTA.
- **Length**: 150-300 words for nurture. Up to 600 for launch.
- **Sending time**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11am recipient local time.

### What Changes from Social
- No hashtags (ever)
- No engagement bait ("what do you think?") — feels weird in email
- More story, less framework (email = intimate, not educational)
- Sign off personally (first name, not brand)
- Reply-to should be a real inbox (builds relationship)

## Carousel (LinkedIn/Instagram)

### Adaptation Rules
- **Tone**: Educational, visual, scannable. Think presentation slides, not prose.
- **Hook**: Slide 1 = headline that promises value. Bold, large text. 5-10 words max.
- **Structure**: 1 idea per slide. 30-50 words max per slide. Bold key phrases.
- **Flow**: Slide 1 (hook) → Slides 2-N (content) → Last slide (CTA/summary)
- **Design**: Consistent brand colors. Simple backgrounds. High contrast text.
- **Length**: 8-12 slides optimal. Under 8 feels thin. Over 12 loses attention.

### Slide Types
| Position | Type | Purpose |
|----------|------|---------|
| Slide 1 | Hook | Stop the scroll. Promise value. |
| Slide 2 | Context | Why this matters. Set up the problem. |
| Slides 3-N | Content | One point per slide. Number them. |
| Second-to-last | Summary | Key takeaway in 1 sentence. |
| Last | CTA | Follow, save, share, visit link. |

### What NOT to Do
- Don't put 200 words on a slide (nobody reads it)
- Don't use tiny fonts (mobile-first, always)
- Don't skip slide 1 hook (it's your thumbnail)
- Don't make a carousel from content that doesn't have a clear framework or steps

## Short Video Script

### Adaptation Rules
- **Tone**: Casual, conversational. Talk like you're explaining to a friend.
- **Hook**: First 3 seconds must stop the scroll. State the payoff immediately.
- **Structure**: Hook (3s) → Context (10s) → Story/meat (30-45s) → Insight (15s) → CTA (5s)
- **Pacing**: Change visual or angle every 5-8 seconds to maintain attention.
- **Length**: 60-90 seconds for educational. 15-30 seconds for quotes/tips.
- **Script style**: Write as spoken. Contractions, pauses, incomplete sentences OK.

### Hook Formulas for Video
- "I was wrong about [thing]."
- "Nobody talks about [specific problem]."
- "[Number] things I wish I knew before [experience]."
- "Stop doing [common practice]. Here's why."
- "The real reason [outcome] happened."

### What NOT to Do
- Don't read a LinkedIn post to camera (boring, different medium)
- Don't start with "Hey guys" or "In this video" (lose them immediately)
- Don't cram 5 points into 60 seconds (1-2 max)
- Don't script word-for-word (sounds robotic). Script the beats, ad-lib the words.
