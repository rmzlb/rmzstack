# Anti-AI Writing Rules

These rules exist because AI-generated text has recognizable patterns that destroy credibility.
Every rule here comes from a real pattern that makes readers feel like they're reading a robot.

Apply these BEFORE writing. Check for them AFTER writing. Fail any draft that breaks 3+.

---

## Typography Bans (Hard Rules)

These are binary. One instance = flag. Two instances = rewrite.

| Element | Status | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| Em-dash (—) | ❌ BANNED | #1 AI signal. Real writers use comma or period. |
| Bullet lists in prose body | ❌ BANNED | Prose should flow. Lists fragment ideas. |
| Bold headers mid-post | ❌ BANNED | Reads like a blog outline, not a human voice |
| Arrow lists (→) | ❌ BANNED | Only exists in AI-generated "frameworks" |
| Excessive exclamation marks | ❌ BANNED | Use sparingly: 0-1 per piece max |
| Serial comma overuse | ⚠️ WATCH | Sign of over-structured thinking |

## Poison Words

Never use these. They are statistically overrepresented in AI output.
If you find them in a draft, replace every single one.

## Structural AI Patterns

These are patterns in how AI organizes content. If your draft has 3+, it reads as AI.

### Structure Patterns to Avoid

**1. Repetitive cadence**
AI tends to write sentences with the same rhythm: Subject → Verb → Object, over and over.
Real writers mix it up. Some sentences are very short. Others meander a bit before landing on
their point, especially when the idea is complex or counter-intuitive.

**2. The "It's Not X. It's Y." contrast**
> "It's not about followers. It's about trust."

This felt fresh in 2019. Now it's a cliché. AI loves this structure.

**3. Rhetorical question + short answer**
> "Why does this matter? Because most founders get it wrong."

One is okay. Three in a row is AI.

**4. Always 3-5 bullet points**
Real humans don't naturally organize everything into exactly 3-5 points. AI does.

**5. Uniform sentence length**
If every sentence is 12-18 words, it's AI. Real writing has short punches. And long, winding sentences that take their time to make a point before finally landing somewhere unexpected.

**6. The "Here's what I learned:" setup**
Followed by a numbered list. Always. It's a template, not a thought.

**7. Perfect aphorism endings**
> "The best time to start was yesterday. The second best time is today."

AI loves to end with a motivational kicker. Real endings are messier and more honest.

## Human Writing Patterns

What makes text sound real. Aim for 4+ of these in every piece.

### Linguistic Patterns
- **Contractions**: "I've", "it's", "you're", "we're" — AI avoids them
- **Hedges when uncertain**: "I think", "maybe", "I'm not sure but..." — AI is always confident
- **Personal opinion + reasoning**: "I disagree because X" not just "some people think"
- **Incomplete thoughts**: "Anyway." or "Yeah." at the end of a paragraph
- **Simple vocabulary**: If a 10-year-old knows the word, use it
- **Sentence fragments. Intentionally.**: Used for emphasis
- **Active voice**: "I built" not "was built by"

### Structural Patterns
- **Variable sentence length**: Mix 3-word sentences with 25-word sentences
- **Different sentence starters**: Not every sentence starts with "I" or "The"
- **1-2 subtle imperfections**: A comma where a period might work. A slightly odd phrasing.
- **Present tense for past events**: "I'm standing in my garage. It's 2am." (creates immediacy)
- **Specificity over generality**: "Tuesday at 3pm" not "recently"
- **Numbers, always**: "7 users" not "a few users", "6 weeks" not "a couple months"

### Voice Patterns
- **Self-deprecation**: At least one moment where you're not the hero
- **Uncertainty**: Admitting you don't have all the answers
- **Strong opinions**: "This is wrong" not "there are different perspectives"
- **Concrete examples**: Every abstract claim has a real example
- **Inner monologue**: "My first thought was: this is stupid."

## Quick Pre-Submit Check

Run this before handing any draft to the audit panel:

```
ANTI-AI PRE-CHECK (30 seconds)
[ ] No em-dashes in the entire piece
[ ] No Tier 1 poison words
[ ] No burned phrases
[ ] Sentence lengths vary (check 5 consecutive sentences)
[ ] At least one contraction
[ ] At least one specific number
[ ] At least one moment of self-deprecation or uncertainty
[ ] Ending is NOT a perfect aphorism
[ ] No "It's not X. It's Y." structure used more than once
[ ] Read first 3 sentences aloud — do they sound like a person?
```

If 3+ boxes fail, rewrite before auditing. The audit panel will fail it anyway.

## Replacement Examples

**Instead of:** "Leverage your network to enhance your growth trajectory."
**Write:** "I called 12 people I hadn't talked to in years. Three became customers."

**Instead of:** "It's pivotal to foster authentic connections."
**Write:** "Most networking is fake. The real stuff happens after the event."

**Instead of:** "In today's digital landscape, content is paramount."
**Write:** "I posted every day for 90 days. Here's what actually happened."

**Instead of:** "Unlock the power of consistent content creation."
**Write:** "Consistency is boring. It works anyway."

**Instead of:** "Here's what I learned → Lesson 1 → Lesson 2 → Lesson 3"
**Write:** "The main thing I got wrong: I was optimizing for the wrong metric."
