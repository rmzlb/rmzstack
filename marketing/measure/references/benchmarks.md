# Industry Benchmarks

Directional benchmarks by platform, audience size, and industry.
Use as a reference point, not a target. Your audience is unique.

**Important caveat**: Benchmarks vary by niche, audience size, content quality,
and posting frequency. A 500-follower account with 8% engagement is doing
better than a 50K account with 2%. Context matters.

## LinkedIn Benchmarks

### By Follower Count
| Followers | Avg Impressions/Post | Avg Engagement Rate | Avg Comments |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------------:|-------------:|
| <1,000 | 200-500 | 5-10% | 3-8 |
| 1,000-5,000 | 500-2,500 | 4-7% | 5-15 |
| 5,000-15,000 | 2,000-8,000 | 3-5% | 10-30 |
| 15,000-50,000 | 5,000-25,000 | 2-4% | 15-50 |
| 50,000+ | 10,000-100,000 | 1.5-3% | 20-100+ |

**Note**: Engagement rate naturally decreases as follower count grows. Don't panic if your rate drops from 8% to 4% as you grow from 1K to 10K followers. That's normal.

## How to Use Benchmarks

1. **Find your category** in the tables above
2. **Compare honestly** — where do you fall?
3. **Identify the gap** — which metric is furthest below "Good"?
4. **Fix one metric at a time** — don't try to improve everything
5. **Recheck monthly** — are you trending toward "Good"?

**Important**: If you're above "Good" on a metric, stop optimizing it.
Focus on your weakest metric for the biggest overall improvement.

**Don't compare yourself to outliers.** A viral post with 500K impressions
is not your benchmark. Your last 20 posts' average IS your benchmark.
Improve against yourself, not against someone else's best day.
