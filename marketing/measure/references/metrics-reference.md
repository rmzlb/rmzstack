# Metrics Reference

Complete glossary by platform. For each: what it is, how to calculate, what it actually tells you.

---

## LinkedIn Metrics

### Impressions
- **What**: Number of times your post appeared in a feed
- **Formula**: Provided by LinkedIn analytics
- **What it tells you**: Reach. High impressions + low engagement = your content reaches people but doesn't resonate. Low impressions = algorithmic suppression (check your hook).
- **Benchmark**: Varies wildly. 2-5% of follower count per post is typical. Viral posts can hit 50x follower count.

### Engagement Rate
- **What**: (Reactions + Comments + Shares + Clicks) / Impressions × 100
- **Formula**: `(total_engagements / impressions) × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Content resonance. The single most important LinkedIn metric. High rate = algorithm pushes to more people.
- **Benchmark**: 2-5% average. >5% strong. >8% potential viral. <2% rethink content.

### Comments
- **What**: Number of comments on your post
- **Formula**: Count (exclude your own replies)
- **What it tells you**: Depth of engagement. Comments > reactions. A post with 50 reactions and 2 comments performed worse than 20 reactions and 15 comments.
- **Why it matters**: Comments trigger notifications to commenters' networks = organic amplification.

### DMs Received
- **What**: Direct messages prompted by your content
- **Formula**: Track manually (LinkedIn doesn't attribute DMs to posts)
- **What it tells you**: Conversion signal. DMs = someone wants to talk, buy, or collaborate. The highest-value metric for business outcomes.
- **Tracking tip**: Ask "what prompted you to reach out?" in every DM.

### Follower Growth Rate
- **What**: Net new followers per period
- **Formula**: `(new_followers - unfollows) / total_followers × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Whether your content attracts the right audience over time.
- **Benchmark**: 1-3% monthly growth for active creators. >5% = something's working very well.

## X/Twitter Metrics

### Impressions
- **What**: Times your tweet was seen (includes timeline, search, profile visits)
- **What it tells you**: Raw reach. Less reliable than LinkedIn because Twitter counts passive scrolling.
- **Benchmark**: 5-20% of follower count per tweet. Threads typically 2-5x single tweets.

### Engagement Rate
- **Formula**: `(likes + replies + retweets + quotes + bookmarks + clicks) / impressions × 100`
- **What it tells you**: How interesting your content is to people who see it.
- **Benchmark**: 1-3% average. >3% strong. >5% viral potential.

### Bookmarks
- **What**: Number of people who saved your tweet
- **What it tells you**: The "save for later" signal. Strong indicator of high-value content. Bookmarks are weighted heavily by the algorithm.
- **Benchmark**: If bookmarks > likes, your content is genuinely useful (people save useful, like polite).

### Quote Retweets
- **What**: Someone shares your tweet with their own commentary
- **What it tells you**: Amplification through endorsement. Better than regular RT because it adds reach to the quoter's audience.
- **Benchmark**: >5 per tweet is strong. >20 = significant amplification.

## Email Metrics

### Open Rate
- **Formula**: `(unique_opens / delivered) × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Subject line effectiveness + sender reputation. Note: Apple Mail Privacy Protection inflates open rates since 2021.
- **Benchmark**: 25-40% for warm lists. >40% = great subject lines + engaged list. <20% = list fatigue or bad subjects.

### Click-Through Rate (CTR)
- **Formula**: `(unique_clicks / delivered) × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Content relevance and CTA effectiveness.
- **Benchmark**: 2-5% for nurture emails. >5% = highly targeted content. <2% = mismatch between subject and content.

### Reply Rate
- **Formula**: `(replies / delivered) × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Personal connection. The best metric for relationship building.
- **Benchmark**: >2% = you're building a community, not just a list. Most newsletters: <0.5%.

### Unsubscribe Rate
- **Formula**: `(unsubscribes / delivered) × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Audience-content mismatch. Some unsubscribes are healthy (wrong people leaving). Spikes indicate a problem.
- **Benchmark**: <0.3% per send = healthy. 0.3-0.5% = watch it. >0.5% = something's wrong.

## Ad Metrics

### Click-Through Rate (CTR)
- **Formula**: `(clicks / impressions) × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Ad relevance to the audience.
- **Benchmark by platform**:
  - Facebook: 0.9-1.5% average, >2% good
  - Google Search: 3-5% average, >8% good
  - LinkedIn Ads: 0.4-0.8% average, >1% good

### Cost Per Click (CPC)
- **Formula**: `total_spend / total_clicks`
- **What it tells you**: How efficiently you're driving traffic.
- **Benchmark**: Varies by industry. B2B SaaS: $2-6 Facebook, $3-8 LinkedIn, $1-4 Google.

### Cost Per Acquisition (CPA)
- **Formula**: `total_spend / total_conversions`
- **What it tells you**: How much it costs to get a customer/signup/lead.
- **Benchmark**: Depends on your unit economics. Rule: CPA should be <1/3 of customer LTV.

### Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
- **Formula**: `revenue_from_ads / ad_spend`
- **What it tells you**: Whether ads are profitable. ROAS of 3x = for every $1 spent, you get $3 back.
- **Benchmark**: >3x sustainable. 1-3x break-even zone. <1x you're losing money.

## Landing Page Metrics

### Bounce Rate
- **Formula**: `single_page_visits / total_visits × 100`
- **What it tells you**: Whether visitors find what they expected. High bounce = messaging mismatch between ad/post and landing page.
- **Benchmark**: 30-50% normal. >60% problem. <30% = great targeting.

### Conversion Rate
- **Formula**: `(conversions / total_visitors) × 100`
- **What it tells you**: How effectively the page turns visitors into leads/customers.
- **Benchmark**: 2-5% average for B2B SaaS. >5% strong. >10% exceptional. <2% optimize.

### Time on Page
- **What it tells you**: Whether people read your content or bounce immediately.
- **Benchmark**: >60 seconds for a landing page = they're reading. <30 seconds = your above-the-fold isn't working.
