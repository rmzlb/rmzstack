# Go-to-Market Launch Checklist

## Pre-Launch (D-60 to D-0)

### Strategy (D-60 to D-45)
```
[ ] ICP finalized and validated with 5+ customer conversations
[ ] 2-3 buyer personas documented with 5 Rings of Buying Insight
[ ] Positioning statement written and tested (10-second test passed)
[ ] Messaging matrix filled (at least 1 persona × 3 funnel stages)
[ ] Top 5 competitors mapped with feature matrix
[ ] Pricing model decided with 3 tiers benchmarked against competitors
[ ] Success metrics defined (what does "good launch" look like in numbers?)
```

### Assets (D-45 to D-30)
```
[ ] Landing page live with: headline, subhead, CTA, social proof, FAQ
[ ] Email capture working (waitlist or early access form)
[ ] Product demo video (2-3 min, shows the "aha moment" in first 30 sec)
[ ] 3-5 social proof elements (testimonials, logos, usage stats from beta)
[ ] Press kit: founder story, product screenshots, key stats
[ ] Comparison page: you vs top 2 alternatives (honest, specific)
```

### Content Pipeline (D-30 to D-15)
```
[ ] 5-7 launch content pieces drafted (use content-engine skill):
    - Founder story post (why we built this)
    - Problem post (the pain we're solving, no product mention)
    - How-it-works post (mechanism, not features)
    - Customer story (beta user result)
    - Contrarian post (why existing solutions fail)
    - Framework post (educational, positions your expertise)
    - Behind-the-scenes post (building in public angle)
[ ] All content audited (content-audit skill, score >= 9.0)
[ ] Email launch sequence written (5-7 emails, see below)
[ ] Social media profiles updated with new positioning
```

### Distribution Prep (D-15 to D-0)
```
[ ] Email list segmented: early access, waitlist, warm network, cold
[ ] 50 personalized outreach messages drafted (for founder direct outreach)
[ ] Community posts prepared: Hacker News, Product Hunt, relevant subreddits
[ ] Partner/friend amplification list: 10-20 people who will share day 1
[ ] Paid ads prepared (if using): Facebook/LinkedIn/Google, $500-2000 test budget
[ ] Analytics tracking verified: UTM parameters, conversion goals, attribution
```

---

## Launch Week (D0 to D+7)

### Day 0: The Push
```
[ ] Product live and tested (smoke test all critical flows)
[ ] Landing page final check (links work, CTA visible, mobile OK)
[ ] Email blast #1 to waitlist: "It's live" (short, direct, one CTA)
[ ] Founder posts on LinkedIn + X (personal, not corporate)
[ ] Product Hunt launch (if applicable): submit 12:01am PT
[ ] Hacker News Show HN (if applicable): post 8-10am ET weekday
```

### Day 1-3: Momentum
```
[ ] Reply to EVERY comment on launch posts (engagement = algorithm signal)
[ ] Email #2 to non-openers: different subject line, same CTA
[ ] Post customer story / first user wins on social
[ ] Direct outreach: 20 personalized messages to ICP contacts
[ ] Monitor: signups, activation rate, first feedback
```

### Day 4-7: Sustain
```
[ ] Email #3: educational content (not sales)
[ ] Social: behind-the-scenes post (what launch week was actually like)
[ ] Follow up with every trial signup who hasn't activated
[ ] Collect and publish first testimonials/feedback
[ ] Retargeting ads live (if using paid)
```

---

## Post-Launch (D+7 to D+30)

### Week 2: Analyze
```
[ ] Review launch metrics against success criteria:
    - Signups vs target
    - Activation rate (% who completed key action)
    - Feedback themes (what do people love? what's confusing?)
[ ] Update messaging based on real user language (use their words, not yours)
[ ] Identify top acquisition channel (where did best users come from?)
[ ] Start content repurposing engine (content-repurpose skill)
```

### Week 3-4: Iterate
```
[ ] Double down on best-performing channel
[ ] Cut or pause underperforming channels
[ ] A/B test: landing page headline (biggest impact test)
[ ] Launch email nurture sequence for non-converters
[ ] Build case study from best early customer
[ ] Plan month 2 content calendar based on what worked
```

## Channel Selection Framework

Not every channel works for every product. Pick 2-3 to start.

| Channel | Best for | Cost | Time to results | Effort |
|---------|----------|------|-----------------|--------|
| Founder LinkedIn | B2B, dev tools, SaaS | Free | 2-4 weeks | Medium |
| Twitter/X | Dev tools, AI, crypto, media | Free | 1-3 months | High |
| Product Hunt | Consumer, dev tools, SaaS | Free | Immediate (spike) | Low |
| Hacker News | Dev tools, infra, open source | Free | Immediate (spike) | Low |
| SEO / Blog | Any with search intent | Free | 3-6 months | High |
| Cold email | B2B, high ACV (>$5K) | Low | 2-4 weeks | Medium |
| LinkedIn Ads | B2B, clear ICP by job title | $$$$ | 2-4 weeks | Medium |
| Facebook/Instagram Ads | B2C, visual products | $$ | 1-2 weeks | Medium |
| Google Ads | High search intent, clear keywords | $$$ | 1-2 weeks | Low |
| Community (Reddit, Discord) | Niche audiences, dev tools | Free | 1-3 months | Medium |
| Partnerships | Complementary products | Free | 1-3 months | High |
| Referral/viral | Products with network effects | Free | 2-6 months | High |

### Decision process:
1. Where does your ICP spend time? (from persona research)
2. What's your budget? ($$$ = paid, $0 = organic + outreach)
3. What's your timeline? (need results in 2 weeks = paid, can wait 3 months = SEO)
4. Pick 2 channels max. Master them before adding more.

## Budget Allocation by Stage

### Pre-revenue / bootstrapped ($0-500/month)
- 80% founder time on organic content + direct outreach
- 20% small ad budget for testing ($100-500) — learn what converts
- Focus: LinkedIn organic, community, cold outreach

### Early revenue / seed ($500-5,000/month)
- 40% content production (may hire freelancer)
- 30% paid acquisition (test 2-3 channels, kill losers fast)
- 20% tools (email, analytics, social scheduling)
- 10% experimental (new channel test each month)

### Growth / Series A ($5,000-25,000/month)
- 30% paid acquisition (scale what works from seed stage)
- 25% content production (in-house or agency)
- 20% SEO investment (if search intent exists)
- 15% tools + automation
- 10% brand (events, sponsorships, PR)

**Rule: never spread budget across more than 3 channels at once.** Better to dominate 2 than be mediocre at 5.
