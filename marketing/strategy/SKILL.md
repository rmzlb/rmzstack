# Marketing Strategy

Strategic foundations before any content or campaign. Use this skill to define WHO you're targeting, WHY they should buy, and HOW to reach them.

## Actions

| Action | What it does |
|---|---|
| `icp` | Build or refine your Ideal Customer Profile |
| `persona` | Create detailed buyer personas with 5 Rings of Buying Insight |
| `positioning` | Apply April Dunford's positioning framework |
| `messaging` | Build a persona × funnel stage messaging matrix |
| `competitive` | Run systematic competitor analysis |
| `gtm` | Build a go-to-market launch plan |

---

## 1. ICP Builder

→ Full template + filled example: `references/icp-template.md`

Quick fields:
- **Firmographics**: company size (headcount + revenue), industry vertical, geography, growth stage
- **Technographics**: current tools, integrations needed, tech maturity
- **Behavioral signals**: what they Google, communities they're in, content they publish
- **Pain signals**: what they complain about, support tickets themes, job postings language
- **Buying process**: who initiates, who blocks, budget cycle, decision timeline

**The Mom Test rule** (Rob Fitzpatrick): When researching ICPs, ask about their past behavior, not their hypothetical future behavior. "What did you last use to solve X?" beats "Would you buy a tool that does X?"

## 2. Buyer Personas

→ Full template + examples: `references/icp-template.md` (Section 2)

5 Rings of Buying Insight (Adele Revella):
1. **Priority Initiatives** — What made them start looking for a solution *right now*?
2. **Success Factors** — What does "winning" look like for them personally and professionally?
3. **Perceived Barriers** — What's holding them back from switching / buying?
4. **Decision Criteria** — What features/factors do they actually evaluate?
5. **Buyer's Journey** — How did they discover, evaluate, and decide? Who was involved?

Each persona needs: role + seniority, goals, frustrations, objections, channels they use, content they consume.

## 3. Positioning Framework

→ Full framework: `references/positioning-frameworks.md`

April Dunford's "Obviously Awesome" — 5 steps:
1. Find competitive alternatives (what they'd do if you didn't exist)
2. Identify unique attributes (what you have that alternatives don't)
3. Map attributes to value (what does each unique attribute *enable*?)
4. Find who cares most (which customers get the most value)
5. Choose a market category (the frame that makes your value obvious)

**Category creation vs. category entry**: If no existing category positions your value clearly, consider creating one. High risk, high reward. See decision tree in `references/positioning-frameworks.md`.

## 4. Messaging Matrix

→ Full matrix + example: `references/messaging-matrix.md`

Structure: **rows = personas**, **columns = funnel stages** (Awareness / Consideration / Decision)

Each cell contains:
- Headline (what you lead with)
- 2-3 supporting points
- Proof point (specific number or story)
- CTA (what you ask them to do)

**Jobs-to-be-done lens**: For each persona, identify the "job" they're hiring your product to do. The messaging should speak to the job, not the features.

## 5. Competitive Analysis

→ Templates + research methodology: `references/competitive-analysis.md`

Four tools:
1. **Feature matrix** — direct comparison of capabilities
2. **Positioning map** — 2×2 grid placing competitors on two axes
3. **Messaging gap analysis** — what no competitor is saying (your opening)
4. **Marketing SWOT** — strengths/weaknesses/opportunities/threats from a positioning lens

Research sources: competitor websites, G2/Capterra reviews, job postings (reveals priorities), their content (reveals messaging), their customers' language on social.

---

## 6. Value Proposition Canvas

Jobs-to-be-done framework (Christensen adapted):
- **Functional job**: the task they're trying to accomplish
- **Emotional job**: how they want to feel
- **Social job**: how they want to be perceived

Map to:
- **Pains** → **Pain relievers** (your features that remove friction)
- **Gains** → **Gain creators** (your features that create upside)

## 7. Go-to-Market Checklist

→ Full checklist + channel selection + budget: `references/gtm-checklist.md`

Three phases:
- **Pre-launch (D-60 to D-0)**: positioning locked, ICP validated, messaging drafted, assets created
- **Launch week (D0 to D+7)**: coordinated push across owned + earned + paid channels
- **Post-launch (D+7 to D+30)**: measure signal, iterate messaging, double down on what's working

## Category Design Principles (Play Bigger adapted)

1. **Name the problem** before naming your solution
2. **Create the category narrative** — the enemy is the old way, not a competitor
3. **Design the ecosystem** — who else wins when you win?
4. **Own the data** — publish the category research, become the authority
